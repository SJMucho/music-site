About

Sarah Mucho is a Brooklyn-based, award-winning singer, musician, songwriter and cabaret artist. She is known as the front person for multiple NYC bands including the The Sometime Boys and System Noise as well as her iconic Ziggy Stardust tribute show which was nominated for a MAC award. A unique and diverse vocalist with musical projects ranging from folk, jazz and Americana to rock, soul and blues, Sarah also plays the guitar, harmonica and several percussive instruments. She has performed hundreds of shows in the NYC/tri-State area as well as Europe.

These days Mucho has a few irons in the fire. Now exploring more of an alt folk-rock, psych realm in her writing, she continues to play with Jay Cowit (drums) and Pete O'Connell (bass) of The Sometime Boys joined by guitarist John Blanton of Wounded Buffalo Theory. Under the name Mucho Music they are bit of a "supergroup", merging the American roots sensibilities of the former with the deep, jammy grooves of the WBT flavor. They are equal parts musician's band and party band, seamlessly blending genres while keeping folks on the dance floor.

Sarah works independently as a solo act, vocal coach and session vocalist, she is also a musical consultant/resident guitar accompanists for The NY Choir Project which has 3-4 performances a year. She continues to write and perform all over town on boats, in bars, under trees, wherever they'll have her.
